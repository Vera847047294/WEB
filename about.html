Our Story

Bringing nature back to the city, one tiny forest at a time

Born From Nature Deficit Disorder

Terra Microscape Studio was founded in 2019 by Elena Martinez, a landscape architect who observed a troubling pattern: her urban clients—busy professionals, exhausted students, apartment dwellers—craved nature but felt they had no space or time for plants.

Research coined this phenomenon "nature deficit disorder," and studies showed its real effects: increased stress, reduced creativity, and diminished well-being. Elena knew there had to be a solution that didn't require a garden, a yard, or even a green thumb.

The answer came in miniature: closed terrariums. Self-sustaining ecosystems that fit on a desk,净化空气, and asked almost nothing in return. She began crafting them for friends and family, who raved about the joy these tiny forests brought to their spaces.

"We don't just sell terrariums. We deliver moments of calm, drops of beauty, and tiny ecosystems that remind us we're all connected to nature—even in the heart of a concrete jungle."

Today, Terra Microscape Studio serves customers across the country, from dorm rooms to corner offices, bringing the healing power of nature to the spaces where people live and work most.

Meet Our Founder

The visionary behind Terra Microscape

Elena Martinez

Founder & Lead Designer

With a master's degree in landscape architecture from Cornell University and over 15 years of experience designing outdoor spaces, Elena pivoted her career toward something she felt was more needed: bringing nature into small indoor spaces.

Her philosophy is simple: nature shouldn't be a luxury for those with yards and gardens. It should be accessible to everyone, everywhere. Every desk, every bedside table, every windowsill deserves a touch of green.

When she's not designing terrariums, Elena can be found hiking Pacific Northwest trails with her rescue dog Moss, experimenting with rare ferns in her home greenhouse, or teaching terrarium-making workshops at local community centers.

Design Philosophy: "Every terrarium is a story. I'm not just arranging plants—I'm creating tiny worlds that spark joy and remind people to pause, breathe, and connect with nature in their daily lives."

What We Stand For

The principles that guide everything we do

Nature First

Every design celebrates the beauty and complexity of the natural world. We never compromise on the living elements that make terrariums magical.

Wellness

We believe access to nature is fundamental to human well-being. Our mission is to democratize plant ownership for the modern urban lifestyle.

Sustainability

From recyclable packaging to sustainably sourced plants, we're committed to minimizing our environmental footprint while maximizing our positive impact.

Our Eco & Social Commitment

Every terrarium purchase makes a difference. We donate $1 from each sale to urban greening projects across the country—supporting community gardens, tree plantings, and green space initiatives in underserved neighborhoods.

Our packaging is 100% recyclable or biodegradable. We use glass containers (infinitely recyclable), sustainably harvested wood decorations, and organic soils whenever possible.

We're certified by the Sustainable Packaging Coalition and partner with suppliers who share our environmental values. Our goal: prove that running a successful business and being kind to the planet aren't mutually exclusive.
